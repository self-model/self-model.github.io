About Us

The Oxford Self-Modelling Group is dedicated to exploring the principles and applications of self-modelling systems in various domains, including artificial intelligence, biology, and cognitive science. Our interdisciplinary team works at the intersection of theory and practice to advance the understanding of self-modelling processes.

Research

Our research spans multiple fields, with a focus on developing models that can adapt, learn, and evolve autonomously. We are particularly interested in how these models can be applied to solve complex problems in real-world environments.

Our Team

Our group consists of researchers from diverse backgrounds, including computer science, biology, and philosophy. Together, we bring a unique perspective to the study of self-modelling systems.
